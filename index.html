Summer Holiday

Cayabsab Beach (Cagayancillo Palawan)

Cayabsab beach is located at Cagayancillo Palawan, Travelling is a great opportunity to discover cultures in different places. There are areas that is hard to reach but some tourists never mind that struggle. The municipality of Cagayancillo has historical sites which serve as evidence of Spanish Colonization.

Capusan Beach (Cuyo Palawan)

Capusan Beach in Cuyo is fast rising to be a kiteboarder’s haven in this small island. Just opposite the municipality’s wharf, it has a wide sandy beach that tapers into a sandbar. The water is pristine and during the Amihan, northwest monsoon (October-March), it’s quite windy.

Arena Island (Narra Palawan)

Palawan's best kept secret, Arena Island is a four-hectare island tucked away from your usual island getaway. Striking the perfect balance of rustic charms and the comforts of modern living, your private sanctuary awaits. Nestled in Narra, a coastal municipality of Palawan, Arena Island sits on a thriving coral reef, home to a diverse marine life and resources.

Honda Bay (Puerto Princesa City)

One of the most well-liked tourist destinations is Honda Bay. Known for its beautiful sand beaches, Puerto Princesa beautiful scenery and coral reef, which is home to a variety of marine life. Puerto Princesa, a tropical haven, features a a large number of stunning islands, islets, and sandbars a popular day trip destination for visitors, these islands have a variety of diving and snorkeling locations where you can the splendor of Puerto Princesa's marine ecology.

Banana Island (Coron Palawan)

Banana Island, is one of the many pristine islands in Bulalacao, Coron, Palawan. It is called Banana Island because it looks like a submerged banana from a distance (however, there are no bananas planted in the island). This island paradise brags powdery white sand and azure clear waters plus a sandbar that goes under water at high tide. It is definitely a good spot for snorkeling (we saw baby sharks during our stay here), and great view of both the sunrise and sunset.

Long Beach (San Vicente Palawan)

The longest white sand beach in the Philippines is found at San Vicente, which lies on the northwest side of the main island of Palawan. It is about three times longer than the newly renovated White Beach on Boracay. The Long Beach, a 14.7 km (9.1 mi) long stretch of golden sand beach in San Vicente, is the municipality's primary draw.

Big Lagoon (Elnido Palawan)

Big Lagoon is the second stop of El Nido Island Hopping Tour A, right after Small Lagoon. Just like any other lagoons in Palawan, Big Lagoon is dotted with dark limestone cliffs. The entrance to the lagoon has shallow water, but the emerald color of the water indicates the deeper areas.
